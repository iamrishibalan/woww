Assigning Roles

The next aspect of planning for a webinar begins with the formation of a core committee to ensure the smooth planning of the webinar

STEP 2

STEP 3

Recruiting the speakers

Recruiting a good speaker is a vital step in the webinar. The speaker can either make or break your webinar. The steps to keep in mind while recruiting the speakers are as follows

STEP 3

Experience

The speaker has any previous experience who could entail speaking at a conference, podcast, a lecture or even at a meeting.

STEP 3

Expertise

The speaker is an expert in a particular topic typically and has a sense of passion for the topic as well. This will be just as important to engage the audience during the presentation

STEP 3

Time management

Since webinars are live events, and need to start and end on time, it is imperative that your speaker has strong time management skill

STEP 3

Availability

Webinar presenters need to be available for: pre-webinar session(s), the live webinar and sometimes a postwebinar session.

STEP 3

Adaptability

The speaker should be adaptable to the various questions asked by the audience. so, it’s important that speaker can answer or divert unexpected question.

STEP 4

Deciding on the platform

With a wide variety of platform options available in the market. Choosing the right one for your webinar is crucial.

STEP 4

Ease of use

The platform should be easy to use by the host, presenter and audience

STEP 4

Audience size

If the webinar is said to have a large attendance, choose a platform that can accommodate enough people. Before you invest in a platform, get a sense of the size of the audience.

STEP 4

Budget

There are free as well as paid platforms. Decide on the needs before purchasing the platform.

STEP 4

Recording option

Many registrants fail to attend the webinar for a number of reasons. In such cases, recording the webinar might be really beneficial. As a result, selecting a webinar platform that includes this feature is the best option.

STEP 4

Engagement tool

Engagement is a central part of a webinar. Most webinar platforms provide chatbox features and a “raise your hand” feature. Select the right platform with wide range of engagement tools.

STEP 4

Mobile utilization

With many people using mobile as the device to attend webinar, one should make sure the mobile compatibility of the platform

STEP 4

Multi-stream capabilities

With the popularity of social media platforms on the rise, webinar hosts might benefit from social media integration. Make sure the platform has multi-stream capabilities across various social medi.

STEP 4

Video and Audio Quality

It is critical to select a webinar platform that offers high-definition streaming with minimal latency.

STEP 5

Prepare the equipment’s

Webinar being conducted online needs minimum devices for the smooth conduction of webinar. They are us follows.

STEP 5

Computer/tablets

Any modern computer is basically good enough for holding webinars. One of the following browsers should be installed on the computer: Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, Safari, Opera.

STEP 5

Stable Internet Connection

The quality of sound and video during webinars is greatly dependent on the Internet connection speed. For a webinar with video broadcasting, this should be not less than 10 Mbps. In cases where no webcam is used, 4 Mbps should suffice.

STEP 5

Microphone

Most computers and laptops have built-in microphones and speakers, but they offer low sound quality accompanied by an echoing effect as a rule.

STEP 5

Webcam

Most of the computers and laptops have got built-in webcams that could be used for holding webinars. But video quality can often be improved considerably by using a standalone webcam.

STEP 5

Lighting

A well-lit room is required for the webinar to have a better video quality. If not available add artificial lights to the room where webinar is being conducted.

STEP 5

Earpiece

To conduct a webinar, any usual 3.5 mm or USB headset can be used. Due to the built-in soundprocessing card, it provides a clearer sound and is effective in noise reduction.
